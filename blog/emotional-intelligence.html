Understanding Emotional Intelligence

The key success on your learning journey

First we need to know: What is emotional intelligence?

Well, emotional intelligence is the ability to manage your own emotions and understand the emotions of people around you. The key to a high EQ (emotional quotient - a measurement to emotional intelligence) includes being self-aware, self-regulating, motivated, empathetic, and having strong interpersonal relationships.

What about IQ?

You've probably heard of IQ before. It's a score based off of a standardised set of tests to assess your logical reasoning. Using logic and information to provide a solution.

While it's nice to have a higher IQ, it's something that can be built over time. Focusing on EQ allows us to foster good relationships, be comfortable asking tutors and other learners questions, and slowly build up.

Why is EQ important?

Understanding our coworkers, friends, colleagues really helps when it comes to conflict management and miscommunication. We can either explode and yell, or we can notice the anger. We can understand that whilst we can't control our emotions, we can control our behaviour. This ability to stay calm under pressure is a valuable asset, and actually also fosters a safer environment for learning. For leaders, this fosters a safer environment for workers to be honest and comfortable with risk-taking and growth.

In Conclusion

High emotional intelligence is an attractive quality to any place which requires group work - which is very often. It also helps create a safer environment for learning, and improvess your own life with self-regulating skills and the ability to deescalate.
