Foundations - A Reflection

  • 3 July 2024
  • |
  • Amy Jackson

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

It feels like identity, values, and ego are all a blind spot for me. I've not overanalysed myself with pen-to-paper, so it's very strange.

However, I've learnt I CAN overcome my anxiety when it troubles me. It's also nice to know that I love coding enough that, even if it gets tough, I'll spend (probably too much time) searching up solutions and finding out new ways to do things.

It's also been nice to learn my weaknesses and how they impact my workplace behaviour; when I took the recommended quiz I was surprised at how accurately it described my struggles.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I would say values sort of manage who I gravitate towards and who I avoid. Empathy and self-awareness are important because, with coding, you're usually going to be working with other people on projects. It's important to foster a good working environment to help with your learning.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

Not too surprising, but it's good to know I still don't like writing about myself and posting it publicly.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Writing about things I'm struggling with was uncomfortable, and writing about my identity and values was really difficult. I'm glad there were recommended quizzes we could take, because I honestly don't think I would know what to write.

I also don't know how long these answers are supposed to be.

And, as usual, practising mindfulness has also been a challenge.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

A lot of it looks like self-regulation and getting past the stress and doubt. Self-regulation is such an important tool in your career and in life. Utilising it during bootcamp is a great way to try out these tools in a safe learning environment, so we can carry them with us.

It's also been mentioned that employers are looking for people with good 'human skills'. Building them up will help with our career outlook.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't think so at all. It's really nailed down my outlook on career prospects and what I value in the people I interact with. Before I joined DevAcademy, I genuinely thought it was a smaller part of the course that basically taught you how to be polite during social interaction. It's gone a lot deeper, in very thoughtful and meaningful ways that I can use outside of DevAcademy, and for that I'm grateful.
