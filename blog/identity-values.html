Identity, Values, and Strengths

Ethical decisions

I decided to support my coworker when she was experiencing workplace bullying. She was new to the country and did not know the laws, and I did not want her to think that behaviour was acceptable. In terms of my employee vs. my coworkers, there is not much weighing to do when someone's mental health and wellbeing are involved - at least in my eyes.

Looking back, I learned a lot about what happens when you speak up and how it can disadvantage you. It made me think about what I value in an employer, and what sort of culture I want amongst my fellow coworkers: being able to speak up if something is wrong without worry of retribution.

Culture and values

I am half Māori half Pakeha, but was raised by my part-Samoan father with 2 siblings. My dad has influenced me with his selflessness and unconditional love, which I am so very grateful for. I grew up in a white school and only gained knowledge of my Māori culture late into my teens, so I feel distanced from it somewhat. I hope I can gain back more of that identity as I grow.

My friends growing up truly raised me to be how I am today, and I am forever grateful to them and their parents for showing me how to exist in this world.

I value being able to support those I love, and growing alongside them.

My Strengths

I took the High 5 Strength Test to find out my strengths and weaknesses. I learnt my strengths include humour, kindness, honesty, and curiosity.

I discussed how these strengths will help me in my learning plan. But I think communicating to the people you're working with in a positive, light-hearted manner will help create an open working environment. I hope my honesty will be helpful when communicating time commitments and project updates to my group and to myself.

Limitations in learning & career development

In the world of tech, there will always be something new to learn. I think that's quite fun, and I expect my curiousity continue on far beyond my current learning journey. I think allowing - but shortening - my distractions would be beneficial. On the flip side, taking a break when I need it. I have enough career experience to find a job, but I'm worried about finding a workplace whose culture has the same values as I do. I don't think this will hinder my learning journey.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension

Sometimes you have to work with people who are just having a bad day and don't want to deal with anything. That's okay! It's good to get a sense of how they're feeling via responses - for example, when I was being taught something at my retail job, I received curt responses and semblance of annoyance. I felt it best to refrain from small talk to lessen their frustration, and carry out the tasks I had as best I could. Offer them a coffee, a favour, whatever they need usually makes it easier for me to work or helps pull them out of that tension. I think I would still use this in the future.
