What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My biggest goal career-wise is to work in a company with a good work environment, and managing the weaknesses I've identified during human skills to keep my work life peaceful. I'm happy to move to achieve this.

While I don't have a particular area in mind, I am interested in the finance sector.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My biggest strength is, when I get stuck into something, I'll keep going until I solve a problem - or at least understand it. This is a double-edged sword.

As for my limitations, I think I struggle to reach out when I'm under pressure. It's important to recognise the anxiety that comes with speaking with others, and still push through.

It will also be important to remember the weaknesses I outlined during foundations, as I know they will crop up under stress. I need to be mindful of them when interacting with other learners and facilitators while under stress.

While working on the calculator stretch, I've come to realise, refactoring is hard for me. If the code works I am very afraid to chop things up.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I've never done well in classrooms and get distracted frequently. If my social anxiety tips over, I find myself unable to actually retain any information.

On days like this, it will be hard, but I will have to go home and wind down, and review all the information there.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to see my self-regulation improve, and getting over social anxieties so I can collaborate and help others.

I think it would be really interesting to see if there's opportunity for leadership or helping to contribute to a positive working environment.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

Willingness to help, even if it isn't tech-related :) For example, Dave helped me with the AT Hop Card discounts for community card holders.

The team also said there was coffee/tea on campus, places to store food, and other quality-of-life things that made me feel more confident.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

Respect facilitators & other learners, showing up on time, and not being too harsh on myself.

I will manage my workload by ensuring I keep up with each week's tasks, utilising my Notion journal, and reaching out to Dave if I am suffering :D

Foundations Learning Plan

  • 24 June 2024
  • |
  • Amy Jackson

My learning plan will vary depending on the topics, but I aim to handle my workload as follows:

  • Log in to my Notion document to set my goals for the day.
  • Study from 10am until 6pm (until bootcamp).
  • Use the Pomodoro Timer technique on topics I find hard to get into.
  • Go back into my Notion document and write if I have achieved my goals, and anything else about my learning.

I also think at the end of the sprint I should go back over the learning competencies to see if I have learnt what I need from the course.

My long term goal/career pathway

My most ideal workplace would have good workplace culture. Personally however, I would love to design/maintain/update software in the finance sector with someone like Xero or PWC. I like designing projects which speeds up long and monotonous processes workers go through.

When looking at careers, my ideal job would offer some or all of the following:

  • Decent work culture.
  • 4-day work week.
  • Remote work, only in-office 2 days of the week if at all.
  • Chance at upskilling OR working with peers who inspire me to continue learning.

Early in my career, it's really mostly important to have a manager who works well with their employees or gives them the trust to work independently.

Learning Strength & Weaknesses

I took the High 5 Strength Test to find out my strengths and weaknesses. I learnt my strengths include humour, kindness, honesty, and curiosity.

I think these will help my learning, especially when interacting with my cohort and facilitators - kindness goes a long way, and appropriate humour helps when you're finding things daunting. Being honest with others regarding time expectations, and to myself, will help keep my goals realistic. Naturally, curiousity will always be a driving factor for my learning, and I'm grateful to have it be considered a strength.

Amongst my weaknesses were perseverance, forgiveness, prudence, and self-regulation.

Looking at that was hard, but it's not wrong. I think I do persevere, but whatever I persevere in, it reallys need to take my attention. I have opened and closed far too many hobbies, and now I know to wait before I buy any tools or materials. This course was a 6-month consideration, for example. I do think my self-regulation and prudence will be tested during this course. As for forgiveness - I don't think that will be an issue for me amongst a group of people who seem to just be keen learners, and faciliatators who come across as very open.

Developing skills during study

I really hope my interpersonal skills grow when bootcamp comes. I often forget words and become anxious, but I want to experience coding in groups and pairs to really envision how my future career might look like.

I also struggle working in classroom environments. A big room full of people and loud noises makes me nervous, so I'm hoping I can learn to regulate myself if things get overwhelming.

Learning with others & dealing with burn out

When working on a project within a group, I think it's best to be honest and manage expectations. If I have told my group I will finish something on Thursday, but I realise I can't, it's best to let them and/or the facilitator know I will need more time.

I have a set schedule I follow, alongside some booked out time (weekly) to ensure I self-regulate, which I have already discussed with staff. I also hope having outside activities (gym) will help me exert some of that stress.

If I notice I'm starting to avoid my schedule, or reducing my use of the Pomodoro Timer technique, I think it would be best to reach out to a facilitator to discuss. It's better to deal with burn out before it happens.

Seeking help & Facilitators

I already have someone who helps me weekly, but I am hopeful a facilitator would be okay with me reaching out. It's nice to get advice or knowledge from someone who understands the course and what I'll be dealing with. If I notice signs of burnout, I will reach out to a facilitator within the week.

Time block outs

I will be booking a room on Wednesdays from 11:30AM - 12:30PM during bootcamp. I also usually gym on Mondays, Thursdays, and Fridays - but these are moveable sessions.
